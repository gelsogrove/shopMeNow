If you are subscribing as a natural person acting outside a purely commercial or professional capacity, you have the right to withdraw from your subscription contract within 14 calendar days of the date the contract was concluded, without giving any reason, in accordance with EU Directive 2011/83/EU and the Spanish LGDCU (Real Decreto Legislativo 1/2007). This Section applies only to the extent that, exceptionally, the subscriber qualifies as a consumer under applicable law; the Platform remains offered exclusively to business operators (Terms of Service, Section 1).

Important exception — immediate start of the Service: at the time of subscription we ask you to expressly request that the Service begin before the expiry of the 14-day withdrawal period. In that case: (a) if you withdraw within the 14 days, you are entitled to a refund of the amount paid, less a proportionate amount for the service already provided up to the time you communicated your withdrawal (art. 14(3) of Directive 2011/83/EU); (b) the right of withdrawal is lost entirely only once the Service has been fully performed with your prior express consent and acknowledgement of the loss of that right (art. 16(a) of Directive 2011/83/EU).

To exercise your right of withdrawal, notify us in writing at echatbotai@gmail.com before the 14-day period expires.

4. Refund Eligibility

4.1 30-Day Satisfaction Guarantee (New Subscribers)

All new Workspace Operators are entitled to a full refund if requested within 30 days of their first paid charge, provided:

  • The request is submitted within 30 calendar days of the first subscription charge.
  • The account has not been terminated for breach of Terms of Service (e.g. fraud, abuse, or IP violations — see Section 6).

Refund amount: the full subscription fee paid for the first billing period. Any usage-based fees, where provided for by the plan, accrued during the 30-day window are non-refundable.

4.2 Billing Errors

You are entitled to a full refund of any erroneous charge, including:

  • Double-billing for the same period.
  • Being charged after cancelling your subscription.
  • Being charged an amount different from the plan price shown at checkout.

Billing error refund requests must be submitted within 30 days of the incorrect charge, with supporting evidence (transaction ID, screenshot). Contact echatbotai@gmail.com.

4.3 Service Outages Exceeding 24 Consecutive Hours

If eChatbot suffers an unplanned platform outage exceeding 24 consecutive hours in any billing month, you are entitled to a pro-rata credit calculated as:

Credit = (Monthly fee ÷ days in billing month) × number of outage days

Credits are applied automatically to your next invoice — no request needed. You will be notified by email with the outage details and credit amount. Planned maintenance windows announced at least 24 hours in advance do not qualify as outages.

4.4 Account Termination by eChatbot Without Cause

If eChatbot terminates your account without cause (i.e. not due to a breach of our Terms of Service on your part), you are entitled to a pro-rata refund for the unused portion of your current billing period.

5. AI Service Limitations — Impact on Refunds

eChatbot provides AI-generated conversational responses "as-is", with commercially reasonable accuracy. Accuracy is not guaranteed. The following rules apply specifically to refunds:

  • No refund for AI content errors. Refunds are not granted on the basis that the AI chatbot delivered incorrect responses to a Merchant's customers — including inaccurate product information, pricing errors, or other content generated by the AI.
  • Merchant review obligation. The Workspace Operator is responsible for reviewing and validating AI-generated content before relying on it commercially. The AI is an assistive tool, not a substitute for human oversight.
  • Customer disputes are the Merchant's responsibility. If an AI response caused a complaint from one of the Merchant's own customers, the Merchant must resolve that matter directly with their customer. eChatbot is not a party to that relationship.
  • Limited liability. In all cases, eChatbot's total liability arising from AI-generated content is limited to the subscription fee paid by the Merchant for the affected billing period, without prejudice to the overall liability cap set out in the Terms of Service, Section 9.
