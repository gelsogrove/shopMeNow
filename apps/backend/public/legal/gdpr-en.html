This page summarises how eChatbot processes personal data under Regulation (EU) 2016/679 ("GDPR") and Spanish data protection law (LOPDGDD). The complete reference document is the Privacy Policy, which prevails in case of discrepancy.

1. Data Controller

eChatbot (a project in development). The controller's full identifying details will be published upon incorporation of the entity. Data protection contact: echatbotai@gmail.com.

2. Roles (two-tier structure)

  • For Platform Operators' account, billing and usage data, eChatbot acts as an independent controller.
  • For End Customers' conversation data, eChatbot acts as a processor under art. 28 GDPR on behalf of the Operator, who is the controller. End Customers exercise their rights through the relevant Operator.

3. Legal Bases

  • Performance of a contract (art. 6(1)(b)) — provision of the Service.
  • Legitimate interest (art. 6(1)(f)) — security, fraud prevention, service improvement.
  • Legal obligation (art. 6(1)(c)) — billing and statutory compliance.

4. Data Retention

  • Chat messages: 90 days.
  • Technical LLM processing logs: 180 days (diagnostics, security and abuse prevention).
  • Billing data: for the period required by applicable tax and accounting law.
  • Permanent deletion after account closure: within 30 days, subject to legal retention obligations.

5. Your Rights

You have the right of access (art. 15), rectification (art. 16), erasure (art. 17), restriction (art. 18), portability (art. 20), objection (art. 21) and not to be subject to solely automated decisions with legal effects (art. 22). To exercise them, write to echatbotai@gmail.com. We respond within one (1) month (art. 12(3) GDPR).

6. AI Processing

Conversation data is never used to train AI models. LLM inference is performed via OpenRouter in Zero Data Retention (ZDR) mode. Full details on sub-processors and international transfers are in the Privacy Policy, Section 6.

7. Supervisory Authority

Any data subject may lodge a complaint with their own competent national supervisory authority. The lead supervisory authority under art. 56 GDPR will be determined based on the controller's main establishment and indicated upon incorporation of the entity.
