Privacy Policy

eChatbot (echatbot.ai)

Version 4.0 — Last Updated: June 2026

1. Introduction

This Privacy Policy explains how eChatbot ("eChatbot", "we", "us", or "our") collects, uses, stores, and protects personal data in connection with the eChatbot platform available at echatbot.ai (the "Platform"). eChatbot is a WhatsApp-based e-commerce SaaS chatbot platform that enables businesses to operate AI-powered conversational shops over WhatsApp and an embeddable chat widget.

We are committed to processing personal data lawfully, fairly, and transparently in accordance with Regulation (EU) 2016/679 (the "GDPR"), the Italian Personal Data Protection Code (Legislative Decree 196/2003 as amended), and Italian Law 132/2025 on artificial intelligence. In line with Article 12 GDPR, this document is written to be concise, transparent, and intelligible.

2. The Two-Layer Structure: Who Controls Your Data

eChatbot operates on two distinct levels, and our legal role differs depending on whose data is being processed. Please read this section carefully, as it determines which parts of this policy apply to you.

2.1 Workspace Operators (Merchants) — eChatbot is the Data Controller

If you are a business, merchant, or professional who signs up at echatbot.ai to run a WhatsApp chatbot for your shop ("Workspace Operator"), eChatbot acts as the data controller for the account, billing, and configuration data you provide to us. Sections 4, 6, and 7 describe how we process your data in this role.

2.2 End-Customers — eChatbot is the Data Processor

If you are an individual who chats with a shop's chatbot via WhatsApp or the web widget ("End-Customer"), the Workspace Operator (the merchant whose shop you are contacting) is the data controller of your personal data. eChatbot acts solely as a data processor on behalf of that Workspace Operator, pursuant to Article 28 GDPR and a Data Processing Agreement (DPA). We process End-Customer data only on the documented instructions of the Workspace Operator and only to deliver the chatbot service. For requests regarding your data as an End-Customer, your primary point of contact is the merchant you were chatting with; however, we will assist that merchant in fulfilling your rights (see Section 10).

3. Definitions

  • Workspace: an isolated tenant environment belonging to a single Workspace Operator. Every database query is filtered by a unique workspaceId, so data from one Workspace is never accessible to another.
  • Data Controller: the party that determines the purposes and means of processing personal data.
  • Data Processor: the party that processes personal data on behalf of, and under the instructions of, the controller.
  • Sub-Processor: a third party engaged by eChatbot to process personal data (see Section 8).

4. Data We Collect from Workspace Operators (We Are Controller)

When you create and operate an account, we collect:

  • Account data: name, email address, password (stored only as a bcrypt hash — never in plain text), and company details.
  • Billing data: PayPal credentials and subscription status. Payment card details are handled by PayPal, not by us.
  • WhatsApp configuration: phone number ID and API keys, which are encrypted at rest using AES-256-GCM.
  • Administrative session data: IP address, user agent (browser/device), and session tokens, collected for authentication and security purposes.

5. Data Processed about End-Customers (We Are Processor)

On behalf of Workspace Operators, we process the following categories of End-Customer data, collected through WhatsApp conversations or the web widget:

  • WhatsApp phone number (mandatory, in E.164 format) — the primary identifier for the conversation.
  • Name, provided during the conversation.
  • Email address (optional), provided during the conversation.
  • Shipping and billing addresses, when placing orders.
  • Conversation history: all messages exchanged with the chatbot.
  • Order data: products, amounts, and payment method type (we do not store card details).
  • Appointment bookings: date, time, and notes.
  • Language preference, auto-detected from the phone number prefix.
  • GDPR consent: timestamp and version of the consent given.
  • Session metadata related to the conversation.

6. How We Use Data and Legal Bases

We process personal data only where we have a valid legal basis under Article 6 GDPR:

  • Delivering the chatbot service (processing End-Customer messages, orders, and bookings): Art. 6(1)(b) — performance of a contract.
  • AI/LLM processing of messages to generate responses: Art. 6(1)(b) — necessary to deliver the service.
  • Security and audit logs (authentication, fraud prevention, system integrity): Art. 6(1)(f) — legitimate interest.
  • Analytics (aggregated, non-individual): Art. 6(1)(f) — legitimate interest.
  • Payment processing for Operator subscriptions: Art. 6(1)(b) — performance of a contract.
  • Tax and accounting records: Art. 6(1)(c) — compliance with a legal obligation.
  • Marketing communications to Operators: Art. 6(1)(a) — consent.
  • WhatsApp opt-in / proactive messaging: Art. 6(1)(a) — consent, combined with Art. 6(1)(b).

7. Artificial Intelligence and Automated Processing

eChatbot uses large language models (LLMs) to understand and respond to conversations. We want to be transparent about how this works:

  • Minimal context principle: the LLM (accessed via OpenRouter) receives only what is necessary to produce a response — typically the conversation context, the customer's name, language preference, and any applicable discount. It does not receive account credentials, billing data, or unrelated information.
  • No training on your data: End-Customer personal data is never used to train AI models. It is processed solely, in-flight, to deliver the immediate response.
  • In-flight processing: the platform processes data asynchronously (via Promises) and does not retain data beyond what is necessary for the service and the retention periods set out in Section 9.
  • Zero Data Retention (ZDR): by default, OpenRouter operates in ZDR mode and does not store prompt content.
  • Right to object to AI-based processing: in accordance with Italian Law 132/2025 and Articles 21–22 GDPR, you have the right to object to processing carried out through automated means and to request human intervention where decisions are based solely on automated processing. Contact us or the relevant Workspace Operator to exercise this right.

8. Sub-Processors and International Transfers

To deliver the service, we rely on the following sub-processors. Where data is transferred outside the European Economic Area (EEA), the transfer is safeguarded by the EU-US Data Privacy Framework (DPF), Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs), or an equivalent Article 46 GDPR mechanism.

  • Meta / WhatsApp Business API (USA) — receives phone numbers and full message content to deliver messages. Messages are end-to-end encrypted in transit; Meta separately processes metadata (IP, device info, delivery status) and is an independent controller for such metadata. Transfer mechanism: EU-US Data Privacy Framework (DPF certified).
  • WasenderAPI — an alternative WhatsApp connectivity provider used by some Workspaces, processing the same categories of data as above. Transfer mechanism: SCCs where applicable.
  • OpenRouter (USA) — routes conversation history and customer context (name, language, discount) to LLM providers such as Anthropic Claude. Operates in Zero Data Retention (ZDR) mode by default — prompt content is not stored. Transfer mechanism: SCCs / DPF.
  • PayPal — processes Operator subscription payments as an independent controller for payment data. See PayPal's Privacy Policy.
  • Google Calendar / Zoom — optional integrations for appointment booking; appointment data is shared only if the Workspace Operator enables these integrations. Transfer mechanism: DPF / SCCs.
  • Cloud hosting provider — infrastructure hosted in the EU and/or US. Transfer mechanism: DPF / SCCs for any US processing.

An up-to-date list of sub-processors is available on request at info@echatbot.ai.

9. Data Retention

We retain personal data only for as long as necessary for the purposes described above, according to the following schedule:

  • Conversation messages: 90 days, then automatically deleted by a scheduled job.
  • Agent / LLM execution logs: 180 days, then automatically deleted.
  • WhatsApp message queue: 90 days.
  • Webhook events: 30 days.
  • Authentication attempt logs: 90 days.
  • Message archives: 6 months, then permanently deleted.
  • Customer profiles: retained while the Workspace is active; soft-deleted on request, with hard deletion completed within 30 days (one backup cycle).
  • Tax and accounting records: 10 years, as required by Italian and EU law.

Hard deletion commitment: when data is deleted — whether by retention schedule or on request — it is first soft-deleted and then permanently and irreversibly hard-deleted within 30 days, in line with our backup rotation cycle.

10. Your Rights

Under the GDPR, you have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Access — obtain confirmation of and a copy of your data.
  • Rectification — correct inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Erasure ("right to be forgotten") — request deletion of your data, subject to legal retention obligations.
  • Portability — receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format.
  • Restriction — limit how we process your data.
  • Objection — object to processing based on legitimate interest, and to AI-based or automated processing (Art. 21–22 GDPR; Italian Law 132/2025).
  • Withdraw consent — where processing is based on consent, withdraw it at any time without affecting prior processing.

End-Customers: because eChatbot acts as processor for your data, please direct rights requests to the Workspace Operator (merchant) you interacted with. We will support that Operator in responding without undue delay.

Workspace Operators: contact us directly using the details in Section 14.

11. Right to Lodge a Complaint

If you believe your data protection rights have been infringed, you have the right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority. In Italy, this is the Garante per la protezione dei dati personali (www.garanteprivacy.it). You may also contact the supervisory authority in your EU country of residence.

12. Security

We implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect personal data, including:

  • Password security: passwords are hashed with bcrypt and never stored in plain text.
  • Encryption at rest: OAuth tokens and WhatsApp API keys are encrypted using AES-256-GCM.
  • Two-factor authentication (2FA): administrators can enable TOTP-based 2FA (e.g. Google Authenticator) or QR-code enrolment. Note: these QR codes are used solely for administrator authentication — they do not collect or expose customer data.
  • WhatsApp connection QR codes: expire after 45 seconds and are automatically purged from the database.
  • Encryption in transit: all API traffic uses TLS 1.2 or higher; WhatsApp messages rely on Meta's end-to-end encryption infrastructure.
  • Multi-tenant isolation: every database query is filtered by workspaceId, ensuring complete data separation between Workspaces.

13. Cookies and Tracking

eChatbot is a SaaS platform and uses only minimal, essential cookies required for authentication and session management. We do not use intrusive advertising or cross-site tracking technologies. Any analytics we perform are aggregated and do not track individuals. For admin sessions, we log the IP address and browser user agent solely for security and fraud prevention purposes (Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR).
