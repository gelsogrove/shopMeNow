5. How We Use Your Data

5.1 WhatsApp Message Delivery Providers

For WhatsApp channel integrations, Platform Users can choose from multiple third-party WhatsApp message providers. Each provider has different infrastructure, compliance certifications and geographic coverage. Users select which provider(s) to use during workspace configuration. Supported WhatsApp providers currently include:

  • Meta Platforms (WhatsApp Business API) — Direct integration with Meta's official API, messages routed through Meta's infrastructure (EU / US)
  • WAAPI — European WhatsApp intermediary provider, operates EU data centres (EU)
  • Additional partners — Other certified WhatsApp message delivery providers available in platform settings

Secret Keys & API Credentials: For each selected WhatsApp provider, Platform Users must supply API keys, access tokens or authentication credentials. These secrets are:

  • Encrypted at rest — stored using AES-256 encryption in our database
  • Never logged or transmitted — credentials are never written to logs, error traces or external monitoring systems
  • Access-controlled — only the workspace admin who configured the provider can view or rotate the credentials
  • Rotated on demand — users can regenerate or replace provider credentials at any time via the platform dashboard
  • Used exclusively for provider communication — credentials are sent only to the chosen provider's servers and nowhere else

5.2 LLM Processing & AI Features

LLM Provider: Unless explicitly configured otherwise, we use OpenRouter as our LLM provider. OpenRouter routes requests to various large language models (GPT-4, Claude, etc.) depending on availability and user configuration.

Data Sent to LLM: To generate intelligent chatbot responses, we send the following to the LLM service:

  • Customer messages and conversation history (last N messages)
  • Merchant's product catalog and pricing information
  • Merchant's custom instructions and business rules
  • Limited customer context (e.g., stated language preference, conversation intent)

LLM Data Retention: OpenRouter may retain inputs/outputs for model improvement and fraud prevention for up to 30 days. See OpenRouter's privacy policy for details. We do NOT have control over LLM data retention.

Your Right to Opt-Out: Merchants can disable AI features and use rule-based responses only, though this limits chatbot capabilities.

5.3 General Data Usage

  • Customer Support: Responding to inquiries, troubleshooting issues
  • Billing & Payments: Invoicing, subscription management, payment processing

7.10 Data Archiving & Deletion Process

Important: Data deletion follows a three-stage process to ensure data recovery, audit compliance, and security:

Stage 1 — User-Initiated Soft Delete (Immediately):

  • When you delete data (messages, account content), the data is marked as "deleted" in our database
  • Data is hidden from your account interface and all queries
  • Data is NOT physically removed from storage — only flagged as deleted
  • You cannot recover or restore data after this point via the UI
  • Duration: 90 days (recoverable by eChatbot support team in disputes)

Stage 2 — Archival (Days 1-90):

  • Soft-deleted data remains in cold storage archive (separate database backup system)
  • Archived data is no longer used for analytics, AI training, or feature functionality
  • Archived data is encrypted separately from active data
  • Purpose: Dispute resolution, chargeback defense, regulatory compliance, technical recovery
  • Timeline: Up to 90 days from soft-delete date

Stage 3 — Hard Deletion (After 90 Days):

  • After 90 days, archived data is permanently and irreversibly deleted
  • Deletion includes: Database purge, backup purge, archive removal
  • Deletion is automated and non-reversible — no manual recovery possible
  • Cryptographic key material is destroyed, making recovery mathematically impossible
  • Compliance: GDPR Article 17 (Right to Erasure) fully satisfied

Exception Retention Periods (Not Soft-Deleted):

  • Order/transaction records: 10 years (legal hold, not qualified for soft-delete)
  • Tax/accounting records: 7-10 years per jurisdiction (legal obligation)
  • Dispute/litigation records: Until case closes + 2 years (legal necessity)

How to Request Immediate Deletion: Email privacy@echatbot.ai with account details. We will:

  1. Verify your identity (within 72 hours)
  2. Initiate soft-delete of all qualifying personal data
  3. Confirm deletion date and 90-day archive period
  4. Provide archival receipt for compliance records

Automatic Deletion: After each retention period expires, data transitions through Stages 1-3 automatically. No manual intervention required from you — our deletion jobs run on schedule.

These retention periods comply with GDPR (Articles 5, 17 & 24), Italian data protection law (GDPR implementation), and international e-commerce regulations.

9. Security & Data Protection Measures

9.1 Two-Factor Authentication (2FA — TOTP with QR Code)

We offer and strongly encourage Time-based One-Time Password (TOTP) 2FA for all platform accounts (compatible with Google Authenticator, Authy, Microsoft Authenticator, etc.). During 2FA setup, users scan a QR code containing their encrypted 2FA secret. The QR code is generated securely on-device and is never transmitted to our servers — only the hash of the secret is stored. TOTP codes are verified with a ±1 window to account for clock drift. QR codes are displayed once during setup and not cached or stored.

9.2 Encryption

  • In Transit: All data transmitted over HTTPS/TLS 1.3 (minimum 256-bit encryption)
  • At Rest: Sensitive data (passwords, API keys, secrets) encrypted with AES-256
  • Database Encryption: PostgreSQL with encrypted columns for secrets

9.3 Access Control

  • Principle of least privilege — staff access only to necessary data
  • Role-based access control (RBAC) — admin, manager, viewer roles
  • Workspace isolation — users cannot access other workspaces' data
  • Audit logs — all admin actions logged and reviewed

9.4 Rate Limiting & DDoS Protection

  • Login attempts: Max 5 failed attempts per 15 minutes → account lockout
  • API requests: Rate limiting per workspace (e.g., 1000 req/min)
  • WhatsApp webhook: Duplicate message prevention, rate enforcement

9.5 Regular Security Audits

  • Automated security scanning (SAST, DAST)
  • Penetration testing (annual third-party assessments)
  • Vulnerability patching (dependencies updated weekly)
  • SOC 2 Type II certification (in progress)

9.6 Incident Response

In case of a data breach, we will:

  • Notify affected users within 72 hours (GDPR requirement)
  • Provide details of the breach and recommended actions
  • Report to data protection authorities if required
  • Document and investigate root cause

9.7 Staff Training

  • Data protection & privacy training for all employees
  • Confidentiality agreements (NDAs) required
  • Background checks for staff with data access

10. Your Rights Under GDPR & Local Law

You have the following rights regarding your personal data:

10.1 Right of Access (Art. 15 GDPR)

You can request a copy of all personal data we hold about you in a standard format. We will provide this within 30 days of your request.

10.2 Right to Rectification (Art. 16 GDPR)

You can correct inaccurate or incomplete data. You can update most information yourself via account settings; contact us for other data.

10.3 Right to Erasure (Art. 17 GDPR — "Right to Be Forgotten")

You can request deletion of your data, except where we have a legal obligation to retain it (e.g., tax records). Note: Chat messages are soft-deleted after 90 days anyway.

10.4 Right to Restrict Processing (Art. 18 GDPR)

You can ask us to stop processing your data while we verify its accuracy or legality.

10.5 Right to Data Portability (Art. 20 GDPR)

You can request your data in a machine-readable format (JSON/CSV) for transfer to another service.

10.6 Right to Object (Art. 21 GDPR)

You can object to processing based on legitimate interest (e.g., marketing analytics). We will cease processing unless we have overriding reasons.

10.7 Automated Decision-Making (Art. 22 GDPR)

We do NOT use fully automated decision-making that produces legal effects on you (e.g., account suspension without human review). We do use automated fraud detection, but humans review flagged cases.

11. Profiling & Automated Decision-Making

11.1 What is Profiling?

Profiling is automated processing of personal data to analyze and evaluate aspects of your personality, interests, behavior, or preferences. eChatbot uses profiling only when you consent to receive personalized marketing communications.

11.2 How We Use Profiling

  • Data analyzed: Geographic location (inferred from IP/phone number), service usage patterns, product interests, purchase history
  • Purpose: Send marketing communications aligned with your demonstrated interests in eChatbot Services
  • Consent: Based on your explicit opt-in checkbox during registration or settings
  • Logic: Statistical analysis of aggregated user behavior to create interest categories (e.g., "interested in E-commerce Chatbots", "high-volume users")

11.3 Safety Guarantees - What Profiling Does NOT Do

eChatbot profiling does NOT:

  • Produce automated legal/significant decisions: Profiling does NOT result in decisions that have legal or similarly significant effects on you (Article 22 GDPR). No account suspension, service termination, or special pricing based solely on profiling.
  • Affect your purchasing ability: You receive the same platform features and pricing regardless of profile category
  • Create permanent discrimination: Profiling data is periodically refreshed and can be individually corrected via account settings
  • Manipulate your behavior: Marketing communications are informational only; you always control your purchase decisions

11.4 Your Profiling Rights

  • Opt-out anytime: Disable profiling in account settings → receive non-personalized marketing only
  • Access your profile: Request details of your profile category and reasoning (Section 10.1)
  • Contest decisions: If you believe profiling caused unfair treatment, file a complaint (Section 12)
