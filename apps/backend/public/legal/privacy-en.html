eChatbot ("eChatbot", "we", "us" or "our") operates echatbot.ai, an AI-powered conversational commerce platform that combines LLM-based chatbot processing, WhatsApp Business API integration (managed service), a multi-tenant e-commerce backend and automated order management (collectively, the "Platform" or "Service").

This Privacy Policy explains, in clear and plain language (Art. 12 GDPR), what personal data we process, why, how we protect it, and the rights you can exercise. It applies to:

  1. Platform Users (Operators / Merchants) — businesses and developers who register an account to run AI sales and support channels.
  2. End Customers — individuals who interact with an Operator's AI assistant via WhatsApp or other connected channels.
  3. Website Visitors — anyone who visits echatbot.ai without an account.

1. Data Controller, Roles & Contact

Data Controller: eChatbot (a project in development). Contact: echatbotai@gmail.com. The controller's full identifying details will be published upon incorporation of the entity, before commercial launch.

eChatbot operates a two-layer role structure:

  • For Platform Users (Operators), eChatbot acts as an independent data controller regarding account, billing and usage data.
  • For End Customer conversation data, eChatbot acts as a data processor under Art. 28 GDPR on behalf of the Operator, who is the controller of that data. Operators determine the purposes and means; eChatbot processes only on documented instructions.

2. Platform Description

eChatbot is not pure SaaS software. It is an AI-powered conversational commerce Platform that integrates: (a) AI processing via Large Language Models; (b) WhatsApp Business API integration as a managed service; (c) a multi-tenant e-commerce backend; and (d) automated order management.

3. Categories of Personal Data We Process

3.1 Platform Users (Operators)

  • Identity & account: name, email, business/workspace name, role.
  • Authentication: bcrypt password hash, TOTP 2FA seed (if enabled), session tokens, API keys.
  • Billing: plan, invoice history, PayPal identifiers, billing status.
  • Technical & usage: IP address, User-Agent, device/OS, feature events, API and error logs.
  • Business content: product catalogues, prices, FAQs, prompt templates, uploaded documents.

3.2 End Customers (processed on behalf of the Operator)

  • WhatsApp phone number and profile name, message content, conversation history, order data and any information voluntarily shared with the AI assistant.

3.3 Site Visitors — Cookies and Tracking Technologies

The echatbot.ai website uses two categories of cookies and similar technologies: (a) strictly necessary technical cookies (session, authentication, language preference), which do not require consent; (b) third-party measurement and tracking tools, managed via Google Tag Manager, which are activated only with your prior consent given through the cookie banner, pursuant to art. 22.2 of the Spanish LSSI (Ley 34/2002) and the GDPR. Consent may be withdrawn at any time with effect for the future.

5. AI Processing & Important AI Disclaimer

In accordance with the transparency obligations of the EU AI Act (Regulation (EU) 2024/1689) and as a statement of liability, please note:

  • Chatbot responses are AI-generated and may contain errors, inaccuracies, or outdated information.
  • eChatbot does not guarantee the accuracy of AI-generated responses.
  • Operators are responsible for verifying AI-generated product information, prices and availability published through their channels.
  • End customers should independently verify critical information (such as prices, availability, and any medical, legal or financial matters) directly with the merchant before acting.
  • eChatbot is not liable for commercial decisions made on the basis of AI-generated responses.
  • You are interacting with an automated AI system, not a human, unless explicitly stated.

No AI training on customer data: customer conversation data is never used to train AI models. AI inference runs through OpenRouter in Zero Data Retention (ZDR) mode.

6. Sub-Processors & International Transfers

  • Meta Platforms (WhatsApp Business API): EU–US Data Privacy Framework (DPF).
  • WasenderAPI: Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs).
  • OpenRouter (LLM inference): Zero Data Retention mode; SCCs.
  • PayPal: independent controller for payment processing.
  • Google Calendar / Zoom: optional integrations, only if enabled by the Operator.

Transfers outside the EEA are protected by the DPF or SCCs as indicated above.

7. Data Retention

  • Chat messages: 90 days.
  • Technical LLM processing logs (which include the content of processed messages): 180 days, solely for diagnostics, security and abuse-prevention purposes, with restricted access.
  • Webhook logs: 30 days.
  • Archives: 6 months.
  • Hard deletion after account closure: within 30 days.

8. Security Measures

  • Password hashing with bcrypt.
  • Sensitive data encryption with AES-256-GCM.
  • TOTP-based two-factor authentication (2FA).
  • Encryption in transit with TLS 1.2+.
  • Strict multi-tenant isolation via mandatory workspaceId filtering on every query.
  • QR codes are used solely for admin 2FA setup and never contain customer data.
  • Personal data breach management: in the event of a personal data breach, we notify the competent supervisory authority and, where required, the data subjects pursuant to arts. 33–34 GDPR. When acting as processor, we notify the controller Operator without undue delay.

10. Your Rights

Under the GDPR you have the right to access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability, objection, and the right not to be subject to solely automated decisions with legal effects. End customers should exercise rights through the relevant Operator, who is the controller; we will assist Operators in responding. To exercise rights, contact echatbotai@gmail.com. We respond to requests within one (1) month of receipt, extendable by two months for complex or numerous requests, with prior information to the data subject (art. 12(3) GDPR).

11. Supervisory Authority & Complaints

Because the Platform serves users across the EU, each data subject may lodge a complaint with their own local Data Protection Authority (for example, the Garante in Italy, the CNPD in Portugal). This does not affect any other administrative or judicial remedy.

The lead supervisory authority under art. 56 GDPR will be determined based on the controller's main establishment and indicated upon incorporation of the entity.
